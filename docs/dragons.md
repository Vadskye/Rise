# Dragons

## Dragon Categorization

### Chromatic/Metallic

This is the classic SRD configuration. Chromatic dragons are always named after a color, and they are evil. Metallic dragons are named after a metal, and they are generally good. Each type of dragon has an associated tag and personality.

Advantages:

* Familiar to some players
* Clearly identifies dragons that are ethically permissible to fight while allowing allied dragons as well
* Clear tag organization

Disadvantages:
* Feels artificial
* Chromatic/metallic is not a "standard" fantasy dichotomy
* Most metals don't have a native association with tags or personality traits, making them hard to remember
* Colors are generally easy to tag, but color-coded personality traits are more ambiguous
* Subtle differences between colors and metals requires a multiplicative complexity of very slightly different dragon statistics
* Ambiguous how much a typical character should know about dragon color/metal, creating metagaming risks

### No standard categorization

There is a standard "dragon" monster which applies to all dragons. Individual dragons can have any tag and any personality.

Advantages:
* Simple to define and reference dragon statistics
* Dragons are all unique

Disadvantages:
* With no standard dragon types, there are no narrative conventions to latch onto
